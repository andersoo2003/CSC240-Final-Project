What Type of Radioisotope do you Use?

In the case of prostate cancer, there are three isotopes that are commonly used: iodine-125, palladium-103, and cesium-131, each with their own and average energies. While there are a wide variety of reasons to use alternative isotopes (i.e., allergies), there are more advantages to choosing an isotope with a shorter half-life:

  • Less time exposed to radiation.
  • The body can resolve many symptoms without the need for medical intervention.

Recent data suggests that palladium isotopes are superior to iodine (both of which have short half-lives) because they may have higher cure rates. These higher cure rates could be due to palladium having a lower average energy which results in a higher number of seeds needing to be placed.
